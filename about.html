About Me

Hello, World!

I completed my undergraduate studies at UCLA in December of 2021, earning a Bachelor of Arts in Communication with a specialization in Computing and a minor in Digital Humanities. Due to my interdisciplinary studies, I love to observe and research the intersection between the social sciences, humanities, and technology. While completing my graduate studies, I do gigs for film and TV productions in Hollywood and NYC as an actor, extra, photo double, and more. As of March 2022, I am eligible to join the Screen Actors Guild. Previously, I interned at Ford Motors as a Software Engineering intern and worked on for the UCLA Information Services - Desktop Support team. My plan for the future is to work in the technology sector, bringing effective solutions to all problems - from macro to micro.

I am a naturalized immigrant to the United States, having been born in South Korea. Growing up in Southern California and continuing my education here, I feel very privileged for the opportunity to have an amazing community and infrastructure around me. Not to forget the great weather. I always try to pass it forward, having received the Presidential Volunteer Service Award for my volunteer work at the SFV Korean Senior Citizen Association and Henry Mayo Newhall Hospital. Previously, I tutored K-12 children in math at the non-profit Opportunities for Learning in Valencia and was a Technology Intern at the Center for the Study of Political Graphics. Throughout the first couple years of the COVID-19 pandemic, I contributed to Los Angeles' A+D Museum as a Digital Volunteer, adding my input to the dialogue of architecture and design.

My hobbies are playing the guitar, following Formula One, traveling the world, discussing architecture, and following Liverpool FCynwa.
